# Elevation Fixture Matrix

This document describes scenario coverage for the elevation model without
publishing private or user-specific track filenames.

## Rules

- Use anonymized scenario ids in documentation.
- Keep real fixture filenames in tests when needed; this document uses scenario
  ids only.
- Every numeric recalibration must name which scenario it protects.
- Synthetic fixtures are allowed when the behavior is structural.
- Real-track fixtures are preferred for tuning tolerances and preventing
  overfitting to synthetic data.

## Scenario Matrix

| Scenario id                     | Activity expectation                       | Source expectation | Expected behavior                                                                                                     | Tolerance policy                                                       | Protected risk                                                  |
| ------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ | ------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| `mountain-loop-low-noise`       | `foot` or high-confidence outdoor movement | GPX or barometric  | Distance, gain, loss, elapsed, and moving time stay close to accepted reference values.                               | Display-rounding tolerance plus explicit metric regression tolerance.  | Prevents over-smoothing real climbs.                            |
| `river-noisy-descent`           | `water` or water-like low-speed descent    | Noisy GPX          | Suppress thousands of meters of false ascent while preserving sustained downstream loss.                              | Wider gain/loss tolerance because vertical GPS is noisy.               | Prevents noisy river elevation from exploding gain.             |
| `long-bike-noisy-low-relief`    | `bike`                                     | Noisy GPX          | Raise gain threshold enough to suppress vertical chatter while preserving long-route relief.                          | Larger tolerance than mountain loop.                                   | Prevents low-relief noisy GPX overcounting.                     |
| `flat-water-or-coastal`         | `water` or ambiguous low-speed movement    | GPX/barometric     | Avoid inventing gain from small oscillations; do not require net descent to classify every water-like track.          | Low expected gain with conservative upper bound.                       | Prevents lake/sea paddling false positives.                     |
| `urban-motor-sparse`            | `motor` or ambiguous motor                 | GPX/terrain        | Preserve distance/time while keeping elevation confidence cautious around sparse points, stops, bridges, and tunnels. | Low expected gain on flat routes.                                      | Prevents urban/sparse logs from looking like terrain routes.    |
| `fast-run-ambiguous-bike`       | `foot` or ambiguous `foot`/`bike`          | GPX/barometric     | Report activity ambiguity instead of silently treating all fast running as cycling.                                   | Activity candidates must include both plausible classes.               | Prevents speed thresholds from hiding ambiguity.                |
| `untimed-recorded-route`        | `route_plan` with low/medium confidence    | GPX/terrain        | Do not infer speed-based activity; keep diagnostics clear that timestamps are missing.                                | Gain/loss tolerance depends on source.                                 | Prevents untimed files from pretending to be recorded movement. |
| `activity-time-gap-speed-break` | Recorded movement                          | Any                | Do not use coordinate jumps across declared time gaps as activity speed samples.                                      | Activity should be selected from continuous speed pairs only.          | Prevents pause/resume gaps from turning slow tracks into motor. |
| `activity-missing-elevation`    | Recorded movement                          | GPX/barometric     | Do not bridge elevation net/raw activity features across missing elevation samples.                                   | Activity should not become `water` only because of a missing gap jump. | Prevents false sustained-descent inference across data holes.   |
| `single-sustained-climb`        | Any non-water recorded movement            | GPX/barometric     | Count a simple one-direction climb from the initial anchor.                                                           | Gain should match start-to-finish delta after threshold confirmation.  | Prevents losing the first confirmed leg.                        |
| `single-sustained-descent`      | `water`, `bike`, `motor`, or `foot`        | GPX/barometric     | Count a simple one-direction descent from the initial anchor.                                                         | Loss should match start-to-finish delta after threshold confirmation.  | Prevents river/downhill undercounting.                          |
| `run-end-unconfirmed-tail`      | Any recorded movement                      | GPX/barometric     | Preserve the current confirmed high/low when the run ends with a small unconfirmed reversal.                          | Gain/loss should use the confirmed extreme, not the final sample.      | Prevents endpoint-biased undercounting.                         |
| `short-confirmed-hill`          | Any recorded movement                      | GPX/barometric     | Keep min/max and chart extrema aligned with confirmed gain/loss on a short real hill or trough.                       | Chart max/min matches user-visible extrema and gain/loss is non-zero.  | Prevents robust raw clamp from hiding confirmed relief.         |
| `source-switch-fragmented`      | Ambiguous/low confidence acceptable        | Mixed sources      | Add confidence penalties when source switches create many short elevation runs.                                       | Confidence level should not be high.                                   | Prevents mixed-source enrichment from looking overconfident.    |
| `time-gap-source-discontinuity` | Any recorded movement                      | GPX/barometric     | Split source-trust scoring on declared time-gap discontinuities.                                                      | Source should not be downgraded only because of the gapped jump.       | Prevents paused/gapped jumps from looking like source noise.    |
| `time-gap-source-subthreshold`  | Any recorded movement                      | GPX/barometric     | Keep sub-60 m declared gap jumps connected for gain/loss but exclude the gapped pair from dense source-noise scoring. | Source should not be downgraded only because of the gapped pair.       | Prevents pause/resume corrections from looking like GPS noise.  |
| `flat-route-8m-spike`           | Any flat/low-relief mode                   | GPX/barometric     | Replace isolated 8-10 m spikes on low-relief tracks.                                                                  | Filtered extrema and gain/loss should stay near the flat profile.      | Prevents the old 12 m floor from preserving visible spikes.     |
| `endpoint-extreme-spike`        | Any recorded movement                      | GPX/barometric     | Replace extreme first/last elevation spikes before distance resampling turns them into slopes.                        | User-visible extrema and gain/loss should stay near the interior run.  | Prevents endpoint spikes from leaking through interpolation.    |
| `short-run-endpoint-extreme`    | Any recorded movement                      | GPX/barometric     | Replace extreme first/last spikes on three-point runs when the interior pair is stable.                               | Gain/loss should not include the endpoint jump.                        | Prevents fragmented short runs from preserving endpoint spikes. |
| `sparse-plausible-endpoint`     | `foot`/`bike` or ambiguous outdoor mode    | GPX/barometric     | Preserve large endpoint-to-interior change when distance/time context makes it plausible.                             | Endpoint cleanup count should remain zero.                             | Prevents real sparse mountain starts from being flattened.      |
| `sparse-mountain-track`         | `foot`/`bike` or ambiguous outdoor mode    | GPX                | Treat large but plausible sparse vertical deltas as real relief, not noise by default.                                | Source trust should remain usable when p95 grade/speed are plausible.  | Prevents sparse logging from being over-penalized.              |
| `lake-kayak-no-net-descent`     | `water` or ambiguous low-speed movement    | GPX/barometric     | Suppress small vertical oscillation without requiring sustained net descent.                                          | Gain should remain below a conservative low-relief upper bound.        | Prevents lake/sea paddling false gain.                          |
| `city-run-fast`                 | `foot` or ambiguous `foot`/`bike`          | GPX/barometric     | Expose fast-run ambiguity instead of silently selecting cycling with high confidence.                                 | Activity candidates include both plausible classes.                    | Prevents speed-only bike inference from hiding uncertainty.     |
